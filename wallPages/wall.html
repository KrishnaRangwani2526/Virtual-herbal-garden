WALL OF MUSEUM

Past

Born from the Atharva Veda, Ayurveda views health as harmony of body, mind, and spirit, making it both preventive and curative. Over centuries, it shaped Tibetan, Greek, Roman, Chinese, and Unani systems, before being suppressed in the colonial era and later revived in modern India through the AYUSH Ministry and global wellness movements.

Present

Recognized by the WHO as a traditional medical system, Ayurveda rests on the Tridosha theory—Vata, Pitta, and Kapha—rooted in the five elements (Earth, Water, Fire, Air, Ether).
